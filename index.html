Too many factors that depend on the same thing come together to make life on Earth possible than for it to be coincidence. For instance, the earth is at the right distance from the sun to have water in its liquid phase, but as it happens that distance is right, given the temperature of the sun, to allow for the right atmospheric composition to be what it is given the gravity of the earth, which depends on its mass, to hold in such an atmosphere (primarily diatomic nitrogen and oxygen gas) and to have an albedo of 0.7. This means it reflects 70% of the incoming radiation back into space, much lower, due to a different composition, and the earth would receive too much radiation to sustain life as we know it, much higher and it would freeze over. Couple that with the ability of the Earth to generate a reasonable magnetic field to protect it from the solar wind and blowing the atmosphere off into space and we have only begun to list a few of the wide array of factors that come together to allow life on this extraordinary planet.
